Tourism in Cuzco

Cuzco, often spelled as Cusco, is the historical heart of Peru, renowned as the former capital of the Inca Empire and a gateway to the mystical Machu Picchu. Nestled in the Andes mountains, it's a city where the past coexists with the present, offering a mesmerizing blend of indigenous Quechua culture, colonial Spanish architecture, and vibrant modern life. The city's narrow, cobblestone streets lead to impressive Inca ruins, exquisite churches, and lively markets brimming with colorful textiles and crafts. Beyond its rich history and architectural marvels, Cuzco is a hub for adventurers looking to explore the Sacred Valley's breathtaking landscapes, engage with welcoming local communities, and delve into ancient traditions and ceremonies that continue to thrive in this enchanting region.
