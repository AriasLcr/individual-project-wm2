Traditional Cuisine

Cuzco's cuisine is a delightful journey through the ancient flavors of the Andes with a hint of Spanish influence. The city takes pride in dishes such as "Cuy al Horno" (oven-roasted guinea pig), a traditional delicacy dating back to pre-Hispanic times. Another staple is "Chiriuchu," a festive dish typically enjoyed during Corpus Christi, featuring a mix of roasted meats, corn, and local cheese. The fusion of indigenous ingredients with colonial culinary practices has given Cuzco a gastronomic identity that is both unique and deeply rooted in its cultural heritage.

Street Food

The streets of Cuzco offer an authentic taste of Peruvian life, with vendors serving up an array of quick and delicious eats. "Anticuchos," skewered and grilled meat, typically beef heart, are a must-try, seasoned to perfection and served with boiled potatoes. For something on the sweeter side, "Picarones" are the city's answer to doughnuts, made from sweet potato and squash, then drizzled with molasses syrup. Exploring the street food scene is not only a culinary adventure but also a way to connect with the daily rhythms of the locals.

Contemporary Flavors

Cuzco is also home to a burgeoning scene of contemporary restaurants that take traditional Peruvian ingredients and present them with a modern twist. This culinary renaissance has given birth to innovative dishes such as quinoa risotto, alpaca tartare, and trout ceviche, which fuse global techniques with local flavors. Dining in these establishments offers a sophisticated experience that pays homage to the past while looking forward to the future of Peruvian cuisine.

Pisco and Beverages

No culinary exploration of Cuzco would be complete without indulging in Peru's national spirit, Pisco. This grape brandy is the star of the famous "Pisco Sour", a cocktail mixed with lime juice, syrup, egg white, and Angostura bitters. For a non-alcoholic treat, "Chicha Morada", a sweet beverage made from purple corn, is a refreshing choice. Both drinks encapsulate the innovative spirit of Cuzco's food scene, blending local traditions with a universal appeal.
