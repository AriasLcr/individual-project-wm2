Exploring the Sacred Valley

The Sacred Valley of the Incas is a must-visit destination for travelers exploring the areas around Cuzco. This fertile valley with its stunning landscapes and ancient ruins, including the iconic sites of Ollantaytambo and Pisac, offers a glimpse into the Incan way of life. The valley is also home to traditional markets where artisans sell their handcrafted goods. Travelers can engage in various activities such as trekking, river rafting, and even paragliding, making the Sacred Valley an exhilarating stop on any Cuzco itinerary.

Journey to Machu Picchu

Arguably the most famous Incan site, Machu Picchu, is accessible from Cuzco by train or by foot via the Inca Trail. This awe-inspiring ancient city set high in the Andes mountains is a testament to Incan architecture and engineering. Hiking the Inca Trail offers stunning views and a rewarding sense of accomplishment as the path unfolds to reveal the majestic city. For those looking for comfort, the train journey provides breathtaking vistas as it winds through the mountainous landscape.
